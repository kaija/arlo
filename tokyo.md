# 5-Day Summer Tokyo Travel Itinerary

## General Tips
- **Transport:** Get a **Suica/Pasmo** IC card for unlimited rides on JR Yamanote, Tokyo Metro, and most buses. For day trips, consider a **JR Tokyo Wide Pass**.
- **Connectivity:** Pocket Wi‑Fi or a local SIM (e.g., SoftBank) is cheap and convenient.
- **Weather:** Summer (July‑August) is hot and humid (≈30‑35 °C). Stay hydrated, carry a portable fan or misting spray, and plan indoor activities during the afternoon lull.
- **Cash vs Card:** Most restaurants and convenience stores accept credit cards, but keep some cash for small vendors and shrine/temple admissions.

## Day 4 – Day Trip (Choose One)

### Option A – Nikko (World Heritage)
- **Travel:** Tobu Nikko Line from Asakusa (or Shinjuku via JR + Tobu). **Nikko All‑Area Pass** ¥2,800.
- **Itinerary:** Toshogu Shrine → Rinno‑ji → Furutasan Shrine → Lunch (yuba dishes) → Shinkyo Bridge.
- **Return:** Late afternoon to Tokyo.

### Option B – Hakone (Onsen & Mt. Fuji Views)
- **Travel:** Odakyu Romancecar (¥2,280) or JR + Hakone Free Pass ¥5,000.
- **Itinerary:** Hakone Open‑Air Museum → Lake Ashi cruise (≈¥1,000) → Ropeway to Owakudani → Onsen ryokan (e.g., Gora Kadan ¥15k‑¥20k incl. dinner & breakfast).
- **Return:** Early evening to Tokyo.

*Food tip:* Try *yuba* in Nikko, *black eggs* in Hakone, and *soba* at a temple café.
